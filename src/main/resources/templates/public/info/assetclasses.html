TWealthbook Asset Classes

Asset Classes
Asset Class Description Liquidity Risk Factors
Cash Most of the time no return or lower interest income in case of saving bank account

Very High Inflation Risk
Fixed Income Returns are usually in the form of promised interest income on underlying instrument

High in most of the cases Interest Rate Risk (Change in interest rates), Credit Risk (Default on payment of interest or capital)
Equity In case of diversified equity (i.e. Mutual Funds, ETFs) returns are usually in line with Index i.e. SENSEX or NIFTY.

In case of individual equity (individual stock) returns depend a lot on future financial performance of the company.

High in most of the cases Market Risk i.e. performance of overall economy, Sector Risk, Individual Company Specific Risk, Geo-Political Risk
Commodity Diversified commodity may act as a inflation hedge

Returns of individual commodity are usually correlated with demand and supply of that commodity

High liquidity in most of the cases Exchange Rate Risk, Risk related to demand and supply of the commodity, Govt Policies etc. change of duty on Gold
Real Estate The Best instrument to hedge Inflation Risk.

Returns of individual property depend on many factors

Need to Consider Lease Income, Expenses, Taxes and Capital Appreciation while computing returns

Very low i.e. may not be converted quickly into cash

Liquidity Risk, Need considerably more capital (than Equity or Fixed Income) to invest
Alternative Investment There are many flavors. From PMS, Trading Portfolio to Private Equity to Coins and Artifacts

Suitable only for qualified investors

 Low in most of the cases Volatile returns,
